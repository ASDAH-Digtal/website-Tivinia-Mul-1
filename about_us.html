About Netball

Netball a sport played by two teams of seven players, usually women or girls, in which goals are scored by throwing a ball through a net hanging from a ring at the top of a pole.is a ball sport played by two teams of seven players. Games are played on a rectangular court with raised goal rings at each end. The object is to score goals from within a defined area, by throwing a ball into a ring attached to a 3.05 metres (10 feet) high post.Netball is similar to basketball although the rules, equipment and team numbers are different. There is no dribbling; no running with the ball; 7 players; ball passed within 3 seconds; ball & basket slightly smaller; no backboard; players designated to certain areas. Basic skills in netball including Passing and receiving (chest pass, shoulder pass, one/two handed passing).Marking a player. Shooting (close/distance) or rebounding (attacking or defending.) Marking a pass/intercepting (centre court players). Footwork and movement landing on one/two feet, pivoting.Netball is a sport that allows both single sex and mixed gender formats and there is no expectation that all activity must provide for both genders.

Purpose of Netball

The purpose of netball is to score more goals than the opposition. Goals are scored when the ball is passed to team members in the goal circle who then shoot the ball through the goal ring. Being able to work togethe as a team of 7 people on the court. Getting the ball from one end to the other in order to score points for our team.

Challengers of the sport Netball

What makes the sport of netball so challenging is that in the game of netball their must be an estimate number of 7 players in one team. Including the positions of Centre, Wing Defence, Defence, Goal keeper, Goal attack, Shooter and Wing Attack.
